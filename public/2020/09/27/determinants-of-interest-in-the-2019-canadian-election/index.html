Determinants of Interest in the 2019 Canadian Election

2020-09-27

Abstract

Voter interest and engagement is crucial to the function of democracy. In this paper, we explore the results of the 2019 Canadian Election Study to gain insight to the determinants of voter interest in the 2019 election cycle. We find that the average stated interest in this election increased with age ad was generally lower amongst young (potentially first-time) voters. These results may have strong implications for future voter engagement strategies and could act as indicators for future trends in the Canadian political landscape.

Dataset

The dataset in use for this project is the results of the online 2019 Canadian Election Study (CES). The CES is a survey of the Canadian population coinciding with each Canadian election year. It provides data on a variety of political and social topics, such as views of democracy, political interest, opinions of current leaders and parties, and topics of interest in the corresponding election cycle. In 2019, the CES was conducted via online and phone-based surveys. In this paper we will examine the data resulting from the online CES.
